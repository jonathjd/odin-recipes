Authentic Sicilian Pasta

This recipe was passed down by generations of residents local to the region of Sicily. For years it was kept as a guarded secret and was strictly forbidden to share this recipe with anybody who was not trained in the cast iron skillet ways. But lately the requirements have become a little more flexible.

To do this pasta justice it is important that you adhere to the following rules.
  • Only use ingredients freshly grown in Vienna
  • The ingredients must be made in a cast iron skillet that is at least 75 years old
  • The entire pasta must be finished in one sitting.
As long as those requirements are followed, let's begin!

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup Fettuccine
  • 2 Tsb Olive Oil
  • 2 Cups Grated Parmesean Cheese
  • 2 Cups Non-Salted Butter
  • 2 Cups Heavy Whipping Cream
  • 1 Ripe Tomato
  • 1 Cup Sliced Bella Mushrooms
  • 2 Garlic Cloves
  • Fresh Parsley
  • 1 Cup Diced Italian Sausage
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Herbs de Providence

Steps

  1. Bring pot of water to boil
  2. Cook fettuccine until al dente
  3. Dice the tomato
  4. In the 75 year old cast iron skillet insert garlic and italian sausage and cook
  5. Once the sauasge is cooked, remove and pour in olive oil, butter, whipping cream
  6. Heat then add in the Parmesean cheese
  7. Add fettuccine
  8. Season with salt, pepper, and Herbs de Providence to taste
  9. Bake pizza in stove oven for 30 minutes at 375 degrees
  10. Enjoy!
